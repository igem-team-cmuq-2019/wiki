Attributions

In this project, the team tested the ability of Cas12a in detecting mutations and initiating a reporting cascade, a hardware device that measures fluorescence was designed and 3D printed, and an analytic software application was programmed to complement the diagnostic process. We would like to thank and acknowledge the help of all the individuals who added significant value to our project those including our institution, Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar which supported our participation in iGEM, our advisor, Dr.Vincent, who helped us in understanding concepts and techniques used, and Khaliji Bank who sponsored the merchandise for our outreaches. We would also like to thank Dr. Jeniffer Doudna for the project inspiration, team Munich for the wiki inspiration, IDT for the nucleic acids and NEB for Cas12a protein. At last, we thank the staff members of CMUQ who helped in-lab supervision, protocol follow-throughs, travel arrangements, and paperwork.

We would like to thank and acknowledge the help of all the individuals who added significant value to our project and simply allowed us to be here!
